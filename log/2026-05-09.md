## Research findings (one-line each)

- **Mortal computation (Hinton)** — reframed by him as hardware-efficiency, not identity. Ororbia/Friston's `ngc-learn` is the JAX BSD-3 fork target for predictive-coding primitives. **No one has shipped substrate-identity at scale.**
- **CLS / continual learning** — Bazhenov sleep-replay (2022-2025) is closest to our consolidation mechanism; CH-HNN (Feb 2025) does cortex/hippocampus split. **None have always-on default mode — all batch consolidation.** That's our wedge.
- **ARC Prize 2025** — TRM (Tiny Recursive Model, 7M params, 45% ARC-AGI-1) beats Gemini 2.5 Pro at fractional compute. Iterative neural refinement is the cheap-compute frontier; pure program synthesis is not winning.
- **Predictive coding + active inference** — AXIOM (VERSES, May 2025, arXiv:2505.24784) is the closest existing match to our thesis: object-centric mixture model + AIF, no neural net, beats DreamerV3 in 10k frames. Fork candidate.
- **Always-on persistent agents** — space largely empty. "Sleep-time" agents from Letta/Anthropic/Google are LLM-on-cron editing text memory. The substrate-as-identity, always-on-DMN, non-LLM combination is unclaimed.

## Wake-Up Test v1 results

5 seeds, pure numpy, ~300 lines. Three substrate components: slow weights (W_action + W_seq), disposition vector (water-layer / volume-transmission analog), global inhibitory top-K gate. Online local Hebbian updates, no backprop, no gradient. Sleep = shuffled replay with noise + episodic-buffer wipe at end.

| Dimension | Result | Mean delta vs control | Pass rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Self-model** (mean expected reward at random positions) | strong signal | +0.159 ± 0.127 | 5/5 at 0.05 threshold |
| **Autobiographical** (next-flavor prediction from W_seq, after wipe) | **STRONG** | +0.818 ± 0.106 | 5/5 |
| **Continuity** (cumulative reward over 50-step horizon) | unstable | +1.23 ± 8.87 | 2/5 |

**Verdict: v1 PARTIAL PASS.**

- Two of three identity dimensions reconstitute cleanly from substrate alone after the episodic buffer is wiped. **Direct refutation of the LLM-stateless-instance frame** for self-model + autobiographical recall, with no anterograde scaffolding.
- The autobiographical result is the load-bearing one: 64-100% accuracy on next-flavor pairs from Wake A, vs control 0-8%. The slow weights survived the wipe and reproduce specific past episodes.
- Continuity-of-process is **not testable in v1's architecture.** The substrate is a pure reactive policy with no intention component; "cumulative reward over horizon" is just self-model run longer. Without an explicit goal/intention vector, there is nothing to "pick up where you left off" with.
- This does NOT meet the manifesto's strict "all three" criterion. It is informative, not validating.

## What we learned

- Local Hebbian updates on slow weights, applied during noisy shuffled replay, **do** consolidate episodic content into a substrate that survives buffer wipe.
- The disposition vector (water-layer analog) drifts cleanly and biases action selection in a stable way. Architectural primitive worth keeping.
- The global inhibitory top-K gate kept sparsity real — softmax over 3 actions with K=2 produced clean choices.
- The architectural gap revealed: **no intention / goal stack means no real continuity test.** v2's job.

## What v2 needs

1. **Intention vector** — a sparse target representation (e.g. desired-flavor preference, or a partial plan) that updates online during Wake A and persists across the gap. This is what "picking up where you left off" requires.
2. **Tiny-transformer + RAG baseline** for direct comparison on autobiographical task. v1 only had a fresh-substrate control; we need to show our substrate matches or beats RAG on episode-specific recall, at <10% the FLOPs.
3. **Multi-day cycles** — 3+ Wake/Sleep loops to test whether identity drift is bounded (substrate stays the same person) or unbounded (substrate becomes someone else over time).
4. **Distractor noise during Wake B** — robustness check.
5. **Goal-directed lucid replay** vs blind random replay as A/B test (the lucid-dreaming hypothesis from today's wild-idea log).
